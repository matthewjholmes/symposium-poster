Overview

About this research

Extreme heat increasingly threatens urban health, yet cooling resources such as residential air conditioning and tree planting may not be distributed equitably across San Francisco. This study examines associations among heatwave exposure, social vulnerability, historical redlining, building age, cooling-related permits, tree planting, and new housing development at the census-tract level.

Heat-adaptation investments were somewhat more common in hotter areas, but historically lower-graded Home Owners’ Loan Corporation neighborhoods had substantially lower adjusted odds of receiving cooling-related building updates.
